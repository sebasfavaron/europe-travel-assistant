# Madrid Travel Guide

## Attractions and Sightseeing

### Museums
The Prado Museum houses one of the world's finest collections of European art. Open Tuesday-Sunday 10am-8pm, Monday 10am-7pm. Admission €15, free for EU citizens under 18. The museum's highlights include works by Velázquez, Goya, and El Greco. The famous Las Meninas by Velázquez is located in room 12. Audio guides available in multiple languages for €4.

The Reina Sofía Museum focuses on modern and contemporary Spanish art. Famous for housing Picasso's Guernica. Open Monday, Wednesday-Saturday 10am-9pm, Sunday 10am-7pm. Closed Tuesdays. Free admission Monday, Wednesday, Friday, Saturday 7-9pm and Sunday 1:30-7pm.

The Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum completes Madrid's Golden Triangle of Art. Features works from the 13th to 20th centuries including pieces by Monet, Picasso, and Van Gogh.

### Historical Sites
The Royal Palace (Palacio Real) is the official residence of the Spanish Royal Family. 3,418 rooms make it the largest functioning Royal Palace in Europe. Open daily 10am-6pm (until 8pm April-September). Admission €13, audio guide €4. The throne room and royal armory are must-sees.

Plaza Mayor is Madrid's central square dating from the 17th century. Surrounded by red buildings with 237 balconies. The Casa de la Panadería features beautiful frescoes. Free to visit. Many cafés and restaurants line the square, though they tend to be tourist-oriented and pricey.

Puerta del Sol is the center of Madrid and kilometer zero for Spanish roads. The famous clock tower chimes in the New Year. The Tío Pepe sign is an iconic Madrid landmark.

### Parks and Outdoor Spaces
Retiro Park (Parque del Retiro) covers 125 hectares in the city center. The Crystal Palace (Palacio de Cristal) built in 1887 is a beautiful glass structure often hosting art exhibitions. Row boats can be rented at the artificial pond. Free admission to the park.

Casa de Campo is Madrid's largest park, perfect for hiking and cycling. The cable car (Teleférico) provides panoramic views of the city. Operating hours vary by season, tickets €6 one-way.

## Restaurants and Food

### Traditional Spanish Cuisine
Sobrino de Botín, established in 1725, is the world's oldest restaurant according to Guinness World Records. Famous for its roast suckling pig (cochinillo) and lamb. Located near Plaza Mayor. Expensive but historic. Reservations essential.

Casa Lucio is famous for its huevos estrellados (broken eggs) with jamón ibérico. A favorite of Spanish celebrities and politicians. Located in La Latina neighborhood. Closed Sundays.

Taberna La Bola has been serving traditional cocido madrileño (Madrid stew) since 1870. This hearty chickpea stew is served in three courses: soup, chickpeas with vegetables, and meat. Only served at lunch. 

### Tapas and Informal Dining
La Latina neighborhood is the heart of Madrid's tapas scene. Mercado de San Miguel is a gourmet food market with high-quality tapas and wines. Open daily 10am-2am. Prices higher than traditional bars but good quality.

Cava Baja street has numerous traditional tapas bars. El Viajero offers rooftop dining with city views. Juana La Loca specializes in tortilla española with many variations.

### Markets and Food Shopping
Mercado de San Antón is a three-floor market with fresh produce downstairs and restaurants upstairs. Great for both shopping and dining.

Mercado de la Paz in Salamanca district is where locals shop for high-quality ingredients.

## Transportation

### Metro System
Madrid Metro has 12 lines covering 293 stations. Single ticket costs €1.50-2, 10-trip ticket €12.20. Metro operates 6am-1:30am (until 2am Fridays-Saturdays). The Tourist Travel Pass offers unlimited travel for 1-7 days.

Line 8 connects the airport to the city center (Nuevos Ministerios). Journey takes about 15 minutes, additional €3 supplement required.

### Buses
EMT buses complement the metro system. Same tickets valid for both. Night buses (búhos) operate when metro is closed.

### Taxis
White taxis with red diagonal stripe. Meter starts at €2.50. Supplements apply for luggage, airport trips, and night service. Uber and Cabify also available.

### Airport Transfer
Aeropuerto T4 is connected by Metro Line 8. Express Bus 203 runs 24/7 between airport and Atocha station for €5. Taxi to city center costs €30-40 depending on traffic.

## Cultural Tips and Etiquette

### Meal Times
Breakfast (desayuno) 8-10am, usually light - café con leche and pastry.
Lunch (comida) 2-4pm, the main meal of the day. Many restaurants close between lunch and dinner.
Dinner (cena) 9:30-11:30pm, usually lighter than lunch.
Restaurants serving dinner before 8:30pm are likely tourist-oriented.

### Siesta Culture
Many shops close 2-5pm for siesta, especially in summer. Banks and offices typically close 2-3pm. Major chains and tourist areas may stay open.

### Tipping
Tipping is not mandatory. Rounding up the bill or leaving 5-10% is appreciated but not expected. Service charge is not added to bills.

### Language
Spanish (Castilian) is the official language. English is spoken in tourist areas but learning basic Spanish phrases is appreciated. "Por favor" (please), "gracias" (thank you), "¿habla inglés?" (do you speak English?).

## Seasonal Information

### Best Time to Visit
Spring (March-May) and Fall (September-November) offer mild weather and fewer crowds. Summers can be very hot (35°C+) but many cultural events take place. Winter is mild but can be rainy.

### Weather
Madrid has a continental Mediterranean climate. Hot summers, mild winters. Little rainfall. Pack layers as temperature can vary significantly between day and night.

### Festivals and Events
San Isidro (May 15) is Madrid's patron saint festival with concerts and cultural events.
Gay Pride (late June/early July) is one of Europe's largest.
Autumn Festival (September-November) features theater, dance, and music.
Christmas markets operate December-January in Plaza Mayor.

## Neighborhoods

### Sol and Opera
The historic center with major tourist attractions. Most expensive area for dining and shopping.

### La Latina
Traditional tapas neighborhood with authentic atmosphere. Gets very crowded on Sundays.

### Malasaña
Hip neighborhood popular with young locals. Alternative bars, vintage shops, and trendy restaurants.

### Chueca
LGBTQ+ friendly district with stylish bars and restaurants. Known for nightlife and fashion.

### Salamanca
Upscale shopping district with luxury boutiques along Calle Serrano. High-end restaurants and cafés.

## Practical Information

### Emergency Numbers
Police: 091
Medical Emergency: 061
Tourist Police: 902 102 112

### Tourist Information
Official tourist offices at Plaza Mayor, Atocha Station, and airport. Free maps and brochures available.

### Money
Euro (€) currency. ATMs widely available. Credit cards accepted in most establishments. Some small bars and shops may be cash-only.

### Shopping Hours
Generally 10am-2pm and 5-8pm Monday-Saturday. Sundays limited opening (noon-5pm) mainly in tourist areas and shopping centers.

### Internet and Phone
Free WiFi available in most hotels, cafés, and restaurants. Tourist SIM cards available at phone shops and airport. EU citizens can use their home data plans without roaming charges.
