# Rome Travel Guide

## Attractions and Sightseeing

### Ancient Rome
The Colosseum (Colosseo) is Rome's most iconic monument. Built AD 70-80, it could hold 50,000 spectators. Book skip-the-line tickets online for €16. Open daily 8:30am-sunset. Audio guide €5.50. The underground areas (ipogei) and upper tiers require special tours for additional €9.

Roman Forum was the center of political, commercial and judicial life in ancient Rome. Combined ticket with Colosseum and Palatine Hill valid for 2 days. Best photographed from Campidoglio viewpoint. Free audio app available.

Palatine Hill offers the best views over the Forum and Circus Maximus. According to legend, this is where Romulus founded Rome. Houses the ruins of imperial palaces. Same ticket as Colosseum/Forum.

Pantheon is the best preserved Roman building, completed AD 128. Free entry but expect queues. The oculus (hole in dome) creates dramatic lighting effects. Mass held on Sundays. Raphael's tomb is here.

### Vatican City
St. Peter's Basilica is the largest church in the world. Free entry but security lines can be long. Climb the dome (€10 for stairs, €8 for elevator + stairs) for panoramic views. Michelangelo's Pietà is behind glass protection.

Vatican Museums house the Sistine Chapel and one of world's greatest art collections. Advance booking essential (€17 online, €20 at door). Open Monday-Saturday 8am-6pm, closed Sundays except last Sunday of month. Audio guide €7.

Sistine Chapel is at the end of the Vatican Museums route. No photos or talking allowed. Michelangelo's ceiling frescoes and Last Judgment are masterpieces. Guided tours provide historical context.

### Fountains and Squares
Trevi Fountain is Rome's largest Baroque fountain. Throw a coin with your right hand over left shoulder to ensure return to Rome. Very crowded, best visited early morning or late evening. Recently restored.

Spanish Steps (Scalinata di Trinità dei Monti) connect Piazza di Spagna with Trinità dei Monti church. 135 steps lined with azaleas in spring. Designer shopping in surrounding streets. Sitting on steps now prohibited.

Piazza Navona features three fountains including Bernini's Fountain of Four Rivers. Built on ancient circus stadium. Surrounded by baroque palaces. Street artists and cafés create lively atmosphere.

Campo de' Fiori hosts morning food market Monday-Saturday. Transforms into nightlife area in evening. Statue of philosopher Giordano Bruno who was burned here in 1600.

### Museums and Galleries
Capitoline Museums are the world's oldest public museums (1471). Two buildings connected by underground tunnel. Features Marcus Aurelius statue and She-wolf sculpture. City views from café terrace.

Borghese Gallery houses Bernini sculptures and Caravaggio paintings. Advance booking mandatory, 2-hour time slots. Located in Villa Borghese park. €15 admission plus €2 booking fee.

## Restaurants and Food

### Traditional Roman Cuisine
Da Enzo al 29 is a tiny family-run trattoria near Campo de' Fiori. Serves authentic Roman dishes like cacio e pepe and amatriciana. No reservations, arrive early. Cash only. Closed Sundays.

Checchino dal 1887 specializes in quinto quarto (offal) cooking, traditional Roman working-class cuisine. Located in Testaccio neighborhood. More upscale and expensive but authentic. Reservations recommended.

Flavio al Velavevodetto in Testaccio serves excellent traditional dishes using modern techniques. Known for carbonara and supplì. Good wine list. Open Tuesday-Sunday.

### Pizza and Street Food
Pizza al taglio (by the slice) is Roman fast food. Pizzarium near Vatican is world-famous for gourmet toppings. Sold by weight, point to what you want.

Supplì are fried rice balls with mozzarella center. Traditional Roman snack, often called "telephone" because the cheese stretches like phone cords.

Maritozzi are sweet buns filled with whipped cream, traditional Roman breakfast pastry.

### Gelato
Giolitti has been serving gelato since 1900. Over 60 flavors available. Located near Pantheon. Tourist favorite but quality authentic.

Fatamorgana offers creative flavors using organic ingredients. Multiple locations. Try flavors like rosemary-honey or white chocolate-lavender.

Come il Latte near Pantheon serves creamy, natural gelato with rotating seasonal flavors. Small portions but intense taste.

### Coffee Culture
Romans drink cappuccino only at breakfast, never after meals. Espresso (called "caffè") is standard afternoon drink. Coffee is consumed standing at bar, sitting costs extra.

Sant'Eustachio il Caffè near Pantheon is legendary for its coffee blend. Order "un caffè" for espresso. The gran caffè comes pre-sweetened.

## Transportation

### Metro and Public Transport
Rome has 3 metro lines (A, B, C) covering main tourist areas. Single ticket €1.50 valid for 100 minutes including transfers. Daily pass €7. Metro operates 5:30am-11:30pm (12:30am Fridays-Saturdays).

Line A serves Vatican (Ottaviano), Spanish Steps (Spagna), Trevi (Barberini).
Line B serves Colosseum (Colosseo), Termini station, EUR district.

ATAC buses complement metro system. Same tickets valid for both. Some buses run all night. Route maps available at metro stations.

### Walking
Rome center is relatively compact and walkable. Comfortable shoes essential due to cobblestones. Many streets are pedestrian-only or restricted traffic zones (ZTL).

### Taxis
White taxis with "TAXI" sign. Fixed rates from airports: €48 to city center from Fiumicino, €30 from Ciampino. Meter starts at €3 (weekdays) or €4.50 (nights/holidays). Uber available but limited.

### Airport Transfer
Leonardo da Vinci (Fiumicino) is main airport, 30km from center. Leonardo Express train runs every 15 minutes to Termini station, 32 minutes journey, €14. Regional trains (FL1) cost €8 but make more stops.

Ciampino airport is smaller, 15km from center. Bus services to Termini station cost €4-6. No direct rail connection.

## Cultural Tips and Etiquette

### Dress Codes
Many churches require covered shoulders and knees. St. Peter's Basilica is strict about dress codes. Bring a shawl or scarf. Long pants recommended for both men and women entering religious sites.

### Meal Times
Breakfast 7-10am: usually cappuccino and cornetto (croissant).
Lunch 12:30-2:30pm: restaurants close between lunch and dinner.
Aperitivo 6-8pm: drinks with small snacks.
Dinner 8-11pm: rarely before 8pm except in tourist areas.

### Tipping
Service charge (coperto) of €1-3 per person often included. Additional tipping not expected but appreciated. Round up bill or leave 5-10% for good service.

### Sunday Closures
Many restaurants and shops close Sundays. Museums have limited hours. Churches remain open but may have restricted visiting during services.

## Safety and Practical Information

### Pickpockets
Common on crowded public transport, tourist areas, and around major attractions. Keep valuables secure, avoid displaying expensive items. Be particularly careful on buses and at Termini station.

### Water
Tap water is safe and free. Rome has hundreds of nasoni (drinking fountains) throughout the city. Carry a water bottle and refill frequently.

### Emergency Numbers
Police: 112 (English speaking)
Medical emergency: 118
Tourist police: 06 4686 2987

### Tourist Passes
Roma Pass offers skip-the-line access to Colosseum and other attractions plus free public transport. 48-hour pass €28, 72-hour pass €38.50. Calculate if worth it based on your itinerary.

### Money
Euro currency. ATMs widely available. Credit cards accepted in most places. Some small establishments prefer cash. Notify bank of travel plans.

## Neighborhoods

### Historic Center
Pantheon, Piazza Navona, Campo de' Fiori area. Most tourist attractions and restaurants. Can be crowded and expensive.

### Trastevere
"Across the Tiber" - charming cobblestone streets, nightlife, authentic restaurants. Popular with young Romans and tourists. Good for evening dining.

### Testaccio
Former slaughterhouse district, now foodie neighborhood. Authentic Roman restaurants, morning market, nightlife. Less touristy.

### Vatican/Prati
Upscale residential area near Vatican. Good restaurants, shopping on Via Cola di Rienzo. More expensive but less chaotic than center.

### Monti
Trendy neighborhood near Colosseum. Boutique shops, wine bars, vintage stores. Mix of locals and tourists. Good for evening aperitivo.

## Day Trip Suggestions

### Ostia Antica
Ancient Roman port city, well-preserved ruins including amphitheater and mosaics. 45 minutes by train from Roma Porta San Paolo. Less crowded alternative to Pompeii.

### Tivoli
Villa d'Este with Renaissance gardens and fountains. Villa Adriana (Hadrian's Villa) with ancient Roman ruins. Both UNESCO World Heritage sites. 1 hour by bus from Rome.

### Castel Gandolfo
Papal summer residence with beautiful gardens overlooking volcanic lake. 40 minutes by train. Gardens open for tours March-October.

## Seasonal Considerations

### Summer (June-August)
Very hot and humid, temperatures often exceed 35°C. Many Romans leave in August. Afternoon siesta more pronounced. Some restaurants close for vacation. Bring sun protection and water.

### Winter (December-February)
Mild but can be rainy. Fewer crowds, shorter days. Christmas season brings nativity scenes and holiday markets. Good time for indoor attractions like museums.

### Spring/Fall
Ideal weather, comfortable temperatures. Higher hotel rates and crowds during Easter week and major holidays. Book accommodations in advance.
